From Turntable to Spotify

How has the music we listen to evolved from the 1950s to nowadays?

Music is an integral part of our lives. On average, people around the world listen to more than an hour of music a day. Alongside the art of music, there have been several eras of musical genres and artists. Since the 1950s, we have seen genres appear and disappear, but also mark generations. The Beatles, the Rolling Stones, Madonna, the Bee Gees, Michael Jackson, or more recently Beyoncé or Drake have been listened to by millions of people from different continents and cultures. But are all these hits, all these artists really different or do we like a particular music?

Our datastory will take you on a journey through the music world, looking at the music that has marked the last decades by listening to the greatest hits, seeing the evolution of pop and rock music through the ages and the evolution of music in terms of its characteristics.

Evolution of pop and rock through decades

Pop, rock, Hip Hop, R&B, Rap, Jazz are all musical genres that we listen to regularly. When we look at these musical genres, only 2 occupy the top charts from the 50's until today, it' s pop and rock. These two musical genres have been among the most listened to for more than 70 years now, but does the pop of the 60's resemble the pop of today. Does the rock of the 60s with artists like the rolling stones sound like the rock of the 2000s with artists like Radiohead? That's the kind of thing we're going to try to find out in this section.

To identify and compare the audio signature of pop and rock music trough years, we propose to use the following features provided by Spotify that were then normalized: Tempo, Valence, Acousticness, Loudness, Energy and Danceability. Play with the buttons at the top and the bottom of the plot to compare two decade, you can switch between genre with pop and rock buttons.

What can we see?
If we look at the values of the different features, some evolve in a rather small way while others go from one extreme to the other. For example, the tempo of music increases significantly with the appearance of genres such as electro and rap music, whose BPM is very high. It's the opposite of what is happening with Acousticness, where today's music relies mainly on electronic instruments such as samplers and synthesizers reducing the acousticity of today's hits.
