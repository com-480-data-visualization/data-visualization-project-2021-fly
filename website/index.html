Evolution of pop and rock through decades

Pop, rock, Hip Hop, R&B, Rap, Jazz are all musical genres that we listen to regularly. When we look at these musical genres, only 2 occupy the top charts from the 50's until today, it' s pop and rock. These two musical genres have been among the most listened to for more than 70 years now, but does the pop of the 60's resemble the pop of today. Does the rock of the 60s with artists like the rolling stones sound like the rock of the 2000s with artists like Radiohead? That's the kind of thing we're going to try to find out in this section.

To identify and compare the audio signature of pop and rock music trough years, we propose to use the following features provided by Spotify that were then normalized: Tempo, Valence, Acousticness, Loudness, Energy and Danceability. Play with the buttons at the top and the bottom of the plot to compare two decade, you can switch between genre with pop and rock buttons. Scroll right to get started.

What can we see?
If we look at the values of the different features, some evolve in a rather small way while others go from one extreme to the other. For example, the tempo of music increases significantly with the appearance of genres such as electro and rap music, whose BPM is very high. It's the opposite of what is happening with Acousticness, where today's music relies mainly on electronic instruments such as samplers and synthesizers reducing the acousticity of today's hits.

More danceable hits...
Throughout the 50's and 60's, genres present on the Billboard were a mishmash of mood music, blues, and adult standards. Come the 70's, a.k.a. The Age of Disco, the average danceability of successful hits went up with the rise of funk music and Bubblegum pop: catchy, upbeat songs that create a hypnotic, danceable feel. Between classic rock and hip hop, the most successful hits of the 80's had variable danceability levels, but the nightclubs started to get more heated in the 90's with the arrival of Electro music, and hip hop becoming more and more geared towards dancing.
The most appreciated tubes of the first decade of the 21st had a lot to do with emo music and indie pop, but Reggaeton and Latin pop hits were among the most danceable ones at the time. In the last 10 years, most of the danceable Billboard 100 hits came out of an association of pop, rap and hip hop . It seems like genres are becoming a thing of the past : for instance, the song 'Old Town Road', from Lil Nas X ft. Billy Ray Cyrus, was one of the most successful hits in the history of the Billboard. Who would've thought that such a risky crossover between country and hip-hop music would break so many records ?

... and more talkative artists
Speechy music often rhymes with rap music. By looking at the evolution of the speechiness feature, we can guess when the public started appreciating 'wordy' music. Between 2000 and 2005, Eminem and Lil' Wayne were often present in the top position, and were joined by Kanye West, Drake and Kendrick Lamar in the following years. Those artists walked in the steps of icons like 2Pac, The Notorious B.I.G., and Ice Cube, who revolutionized rap and hip hop music during the 90's.
Starting from the mid-2000's, average danceability and speechiness values more or less follow each other: when observed as a whole, music speechiness throughout the last decades shows how rap music, as a genre that started at block parties in New York City, evolved into mainstream hip hop, and is still rising in popularity nowadays.

Key achievements and milestones in the Billboard Hot 100 chart

What makes a hit... a hit ?

We don't have access to the secret recipe to fame and glory yet, but one thing is undeniable: songs that last in the Billboard Hot 100 chart for several weeks definitely figured it out.

Scroll right to discover a radial line chart that displays the top artists, albums and songs which lasted the most number of weeks on the Billboard 100. One hundred positions might be considered as a big difference, so the visualization offers the possibility of looking at the top hits and artists which lasted the longest at position number 1 versus the ones that lasted the longest at all rankings combined.

Coincidence ?
The top songs that lasted the longest at position number 1 all feature two or more artists: it seems like the more artists collaborate on a song, the more fame it gains.
Another striking fact: the data spans all hot 100 charts from 1958 to 2019. In the age of social media and hyper-connectivity, one would expect trends to change faster than the olden days. However, most top albums and songs in this chart (75% precisely) occur in the last 10 years of the data, which would indicate that hits actually last longer on the charts nowadays.
